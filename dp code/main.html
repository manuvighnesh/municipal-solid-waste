CATEGORY

"Types of Conversion Methods"

Anaerobic Digestion

MSW is decomposed by microorganisms in the absence of oxygen, producing biogas (methane and carbon dioxide) and digestate. Biogas can be used directly as fuel or upgraded to biomethane for transportation or injection into natural gas pipelines

Pyrolysis

MSW is heated in the absence of oxygen, resulting in the breakdown of organic materials into bio-oil, syngas, and char. Bio-oil and syngas can be further processed into biofuels.

Gasification

MSW is converted into syngas (mixture of hydrogen and carbon monoxide) by reacting with oxygen and/or steam at high temperatures. Syngas can be used to produce biofuels like ethanol or synthetic diesel.

Fermentation

Organic materials in MSW are broken down by microorganisms into alcohol or organic acids, which can then be further processed into biofuels like ethanol or biodiesel.
